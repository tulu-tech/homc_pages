Understanding 2D, 3D and 4D Rollers

Don't let the marketing terms confuse you. The "D" in massage chairs refers to exactly how the internal mechanical robotic hands (the rollers) can move along your spine.

2D Massage

Rollers move Up & Down and Left & Right. Good for basic, surface-level relaxation. Found in Entry-Level.

3D Massage

Rollers press In & Out (Depth). You can adjust how hard the chair presses into your back. Found in Mid-Tier.

4D Human-Like

Adds Variable Rhythm & Speed. The rollers slow down over tight knots, mimicking a real massage therapist. Found in Flagships.

Don't think of it as buying furniture. Think of it as pre-paying for a decade of daily deep tissue therapy.

How to Reframe Your Budget

We encourage our clients to reframe how they look at a massage chair's price tag. If a quality massage costs $100 plus tip, a single weekly session adds up to over $5,000 a year.

A premium massage chair pays for itself rapidly, but only if you use it.

The biggest mistake you can make isn't overspending—it's buying a chair that doesn't fit your body or target your specific pain points, leaving a compromise sitting unused in your living room.
